C) HYDROPONICS (FROM GREECE DOCS)
==================================================

==================================================
Q: What is hydroponics / hydroponic gardening?
==================================================
### Short
Growing plants without soil, using nutrient water.

### Full (from your documents)
Hydroponics grows plants without soil. Nutrients are provided through water.

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: How does hydroponics differ from traditional soil-based agriculture?
==================================================
### Short
No soil; nutrients in water; controlled environment.

### Full
Hydroponics:
- Uses water as the nutrient medium
- Often uses controlled microclimate and artificial light
Traditional farming:
- Uses soil for nutrients and structure
- Depends more on weather and soil conditions

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: What are the advantages of hydroponic systems compared to soil-based methods?
==================================================
### Short
Less water, fewer pesticides, faster growth.

### Full (from your documents)
Advantages include:
- Maximizes space (vertical farming possible)
- Uses about 20× less water
- Uses fewer pesticides (no soil diseases)
- Less affected by weather (controlled microclimate)
- Faster growth and higher production

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: Can you explain the basic principles behind hydroponic systems and how plants receive nutrients?
==================================================
### Short
Nutrients are dissolved in water and reach roots directly.

### Full (from your documents)
Plants receive nutrients through the water reservoir. A pump oxygenates the water so roots can breathe and grow properly.

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: Who can help set up a hydroponic garden?
==================================================
### Short
An agricultural engineer specialized in hydroponics.

### Full (from your documents)
Consult an agricultural engineer specializing in hydroponics.

==================================================
Q: What equipment is needed for hydroponics?
==================================================
### Short
Reservoir boxes, pump, meter, lights, pots, nutrients.

### Full (from your documents)
Materials include:
- Polystyrene boxes (water reservoirs)
- Water pump
- Micro plants
- Combo meter (pH, temperature, conductivity, dissolved solids)
- LED grow light
- Cups/net pots
- Fertilizer nutrients (N, P, K, Ca, Mg)
- Water

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: How do you set up a hydroponic garden?
==================================================
### Short
7 steps: water → pH → nutrients → pump → pots → plants → lights.

### Full (from your documents)
1) Fill boxes with water  
2) Measure pH (optimal 5.5–6)  
3) Add nutrients and check conductivity (8–9)  
4) Install and turn on water pump (oxygen for roots)  
5) Drill holes in cups for pots  
6) Place micro plants so roots reach water  
7) Use LED lights to support growth

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: Why are water pumps important in hydroponics?
==================================================
### Short
They oxygenate roots.

### Full (from your documents)
Roots need oxygen. In soil, air pockets provide it. In hydroponics, a pump oxygenates the container.

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: Why is fertilizer important in hydroponics?
==================================================
### Short
Plants still need nutrients.

### Full (from your documents)
Hydroponic plants require nutrients (magnesium, phosphorus, calcium, etc.). Fertilizer is added to the water to keep plants healthy.

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: What should you do after setting up a hydroponic garden?
==================================================
### Short
Monitor pH and conductivity.

### Full (from your documents)
Every three days:
- Check pH and conductivity
- Add water or nutrients if needed

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: How fast do hydroponic plants grow?
==================================================
### Short
Often faster; depends on nutrients.

### Full (from your documents)
Organic hydroponics can be slower. If you increase fertilizers, plants can grow faster.
