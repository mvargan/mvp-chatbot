GREEN SKILLS AND JOBS IN ITALY
What do we mean with “Green Jobs”?
With this term we refer to specialized professional figures and the creation of jobs in sectors such as manufacturing, agriculture, management of mobility services, city planning, construction industry, tourism, research and development. The aim of these jobs is promoting a new approach to the labour market where biodiversity protection, a more efficient use of resources and the reduction of pollutant emissions do not necessarily result in the worsening of the quality of life, or loss of jobs. Green Jobs are jobs that guide the transition process from traditional Brown Economy model to Green Economy.
What positive effects would/will green jobs have?
The will increase an efficient consumption of energy and raw materials.
They will limit greenhouse gas emissions.
They will minimize waste and contamination.
They will protect and restore ecosystems.
They will contribute to adaption to climate change.
What are the major damages caused to the Earth by humans?
Human activities increasingly influence the Earth's climate and temperature by burning fossil fuels and cutting down rainforests. This adds enormous quantities of greenhouse gases to those naturally present in the atmosphere, increasing the greenhouse effect and global warming.
What are the most recommended sustainable materials for these jobs?
The list of possible natural insulating materials includes, for example, materials of animal origin or from food production.
What skills are required by companies?
The skills that are required by companies are: aptitude for energy saving through recycling and positive attitude towards environmental sustainability. In addition to these two basic skills, the following skills are also required: problem solving, ability to manage innovative solutions, mathematical and computer skills.
What are the most demanded green jobs in Italy?
Some of the most demanded green jobs in Italy are: Designers or installers of photovoltaic solar systems; Designers or installers of wind power plants; Environmental engineer, etc.
What is the situation of Green Jobs in Italy?
According to the latest GreenItaly report published annually by Unioncamere and the Symbola Foundation with the patronage of the Ministry of the Environment and Energy Security, in Italy there are 3.2 million green workers, equal to 13.9 % of employed people.
The same report also positions us as first in the ranking “in the circular economy” with the highest percentage of recycling on all waste: 83.4%.
Why is sustainable construction important?
Sustainable construction is important because the effects of climate change have already been felt across the globe. Sustainability is important for a variety of reasons, including a better quality of life and environmental quality. In order to have healthy communities we need to have clean air, natural resources and a non-toxic environment. With the advancements in technology and sustainable materials, the construction industry can lead the way for greener projects.
What are the Challenges of Sustainable Construction?
Sustainable construction comes with a multitude of benefits, nevertheless switching to a more sustainable process takes time and money. Moreover, training needs to be implemented in order to start practising sustainable methods. Another obstacle that many companies may come across is the actual cost of sustainable construction.
How are Green Jobs distributed on the Italian territory?
The updated picture of the commitment of the Italian production system in the green transition reveals that at a territorial level, the north-west is the area with the highest number of planned green activations. But, the most significant growth rate is recorded in the centre, while the South grows less than the national average.
