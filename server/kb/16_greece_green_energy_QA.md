Q: What is green energy?
==================================================

### Short
Energy that does not harm the environment.

### Full
Green energy comes from natural sources and is produced in ways that do not damage the environment.
Examples include solar, wind, hydro, geothermal and bioenergy.

HISTORICAL CONTRIBUTIONS
==================================================

==================================================
Q: Is there a famous Greek who contributed to green energy?
==================================================

### Short
Yes, Archimedes.

### Full
Archimedes of Syracuse (c. 287 – c. 212 BC) was an Ancient Greek mathematician, physicist, engineer and inventor.
According to legend, he used mirrors arranged as a parabolic reflector to focus sunlight and burn Roman ships — an early example of solar energy application.

--------------------------------------------------

### Full

==================================================
Q: How was the parabolic reflector invented by Archimedes and how is it applicable today?
==================================================

### Short
He focused sunlight using mirrors; today it collects energy.

### Full
Legend states that Archimedes arranged mirrors in a parabolic shape to concentrate sunlight.

Today, parabolic reflectors are used in:
- Solar power plants
- Satellite dishes
- Telescopes
- Acoustic systems

EARLY ELECTRIC CAR IN GREECE
==================================================

==================================================
Q: What is known about the first electric car produced in Greece?
==================================================

### Short
The ENFIELD electric car built in Syros.

### Full
The ENFIELD electric microcar was built in Syros, Greece, in a shipyard.
It was designed by Konstantinos Adraktas and featured:

- 8.2 hp electric motor
- Top speed: 80 km/h
- Range: 80 km
- Length: 2.7 meters
- Lead-acid batteries

Only 112 units were produced due to political and economic limitations.
