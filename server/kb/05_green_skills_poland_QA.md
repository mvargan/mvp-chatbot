Q: What are green skills?
==================================================

### Short
Green skills support the transition to a sustainable economy.

### Full
Green skills are competences that support green transformation and help achieve climate neutrality by 2050. They promote environmentally balanced production and consumption. Green skills are connected to the Sustainable Development Goals for the period 2015–2030, especially Goals 9 and 12.

Q: What does Sustainable Development Goal 9 include?
==================================================

### Short
Making industries and infrastructure sustainable.

### Full
Sustainable Development Goal 9 focuses on upgrading infrastructure and modernizing industries to become sustainable. It promotes resource efficiency and the adoption of clean and environmentally friendly technologies by 2030.

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: What does Sustainable Development Goal 12 include?
==================================================

### Short
Sustainable use of natural resources.

### Full
Sustainable Development Goal 12 aims to ensure sustainable management and efficient use of natural resources by 2030.

Q: What are Green Jobs?
==================================================

### Short
Jobs that help protect or restore the environment.

### Full
According to the International Labour Organization, green jobs are professions that contribute to maintaining or restoring the environment to a healthy state, before excessive degradation.

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: What do Green Jobs affect?
==================================================

### Short
They reduce pollution and improve efficiency.

### Full
Green jobs:
- Increase energy and raw material efficiency.
- Reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
- Minimize waste and pollution.
- Protect and restore ecosystems.
- Support adaptation to climate change.

Q: What are examples of green jobs?
==================================================

### Short
Energy auditors, ESG managers and decarbonization specialists.

### Full
Examples of green jobs include:
- Energy auditors
- ESG managers
- Decarbonization managers and specialists

==================================================
Q: What does an energy auditor do?
==================================================

### Short
Finds energy waste and recommends savings.

### Full
Energy auditors identify energy waste sources such as inefficient lighting, poor insulation or outdated appliances. They develop and recommend strategies to improve energy efficiency.

### Example
An energy auditor may analyze a school building and suggest LED lighting upgrades.

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: What does ESG stand for?
==================================================

### Short
Environmental, Social and Governance.

### Full
ESG stands for Environmental, Social and Governance. It is a framework used to evaluate a company’s sustainability, ethical standards and social responsibility.

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: What is decarbonization?
==================================================

### Short
Reducing carbon emissions.

### Full
Decarbonization is the process of reducing and eliminating carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gas emissions from the atmosphere to combat climate change.

--------------------------------------------------

==================================================
Q: What does energy transformation mean?
==================================================

### Short
Changing one type of energy into another.

### Full
Energy transformation occurs when energy changes form, such as from chemical to electrical or thermal energy. Types of energy include electrical, thermal, nuclear, mechanical, electromagnetic, sound and chemical energy.
