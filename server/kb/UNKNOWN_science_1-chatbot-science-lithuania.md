What is the role of biology science?
Biology science is focusing on protecting the environment and sustainability, helps to reduce the effects of climate change, studies soil, plants, animals and helps them to survive.
What professions does biology science include?
Biology science includes professions like biological systems engineer, biotechnologist, plant biotechnologist, microbiologist, zoologist, marine biologist, ornithologist, botanist, research scientist and etc.
What does a biological systems engineer do?
Does experiments to solve problems such as developing renewable biofuels and bioenergy, developing sustainable biomaterials and bioproducts and etc. Biosystems engineers are working towards environmental resiliency and sustainability, they ensure there is clean water, renewable energy and a healthy environment, solutions to technical problems.
What does a biotechnologist do?
Biotechnologists makes the application of plants and other photosynthetic organisms by experiments. They are also aiming to improve agricultural crops.
What does a microbiologist do?
Microbiologists solves a range of problems affecting our health, the environment, climate, food and agriculture. They monitor, identifies and tracks microorganisms in a range of environments. They also develop products such as enzymes, vitamins, hormones, grow microbial cultures and etc.
What does a zoologist do?
Zoologists research on various aspects of animals’ behaviour, physiology, genetics, and ecology. They carry out experiments, collects data, and analyses results, study animal’s population to determine their distribution, abundance, and trends over time. Zoologist also work to develop strategies to protect endangered species and their habitats.
What does a marine biologist do?
Marine biologists research for sea animals and plants, studies how they adapt, explores the skeletal remains. They give special attention to marine ecosystems, particularly to the understanding of food chains and predator-prey relationship, concerns with the effects of oceans pollution, pesticide and fertilizer runoff from land, oil spills and silting from coastline construction activities.
What does an ornithologist do?
Ornithologists conducts research with specimens in a laboratory or out in the field, where they study birds in their natural environments. They count birds due to their migration and if necessary, helps to make nests, gives food, nurses and treats them.
What does an ecologist do?
Identifies ecological and climate change problems and proposes ways to solve them.
What does a farmer do?
A farmer is responsible for food production; Environmental stewardship/sustainable farming; Economic development and rural development.
What is a vertical farm?
It is a building in which crops are grown commercially in multiple layers or levels.
