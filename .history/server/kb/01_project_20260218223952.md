S.O.S. Erasmus Project: STEAM for Sustainability
Short (bullets)
This is an Erasmus+ project about STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, Maths) and sustainability.

The project connects schools from Croatia, Italy, Greece, Lithuania, and Poland.
The goal is to help students learn about sustainability through STEAM activities and simple digital tools (including this chatbot).
Long
The “STEAM for Sustainability” Erasmus+ project brings together partner schools from five countries. Students and teachers work on sustainability topics using STEAM approaches. The chatbot is part of the project and helps students ask questions and learn using simple English.

Partners
Croatia — Gimnazija Josipa Slavenskog Čakovec
Short (bullets)
A grammar school in Čakovec, Croatia with 623 students and 61 teachers.
Offers four academic programmes: humanities, mathematics, science, and modern languages.

Located in Međimurje county, near Slovenia and Hungary, close to Austria.
Long
Gimnazija Josipa Slavenskog Čakovec is a well-performing grammar school preparing students for university. It serves a region of about 110,000 inhabitants in northern Croatia. The school is proud of strong results in state-level exams.

Italy — I.T.E.T. Leonardo da Vinci (Milazzo)
Short (bullets)
A technological high school in Milazzo, Italy with almost 1,000 students.
Offers many courses: business, IT, tourism, construction/territory, graphics/communication, and transport/logistics.

Milazzo is a historic seaside town and a gateway to the Aeolian Islands.
Long
ITET “Leonardo da Vinci” started as an accountancy course in 1956, and later expanded into many technical and vocational directions. It focuses on student motivation and local needs. The school is located on a peninsula surrounded by the sea, with rich history and natural beauty.
